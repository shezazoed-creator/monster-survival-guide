"Liminal" comes from the Latin _limen_, meaning threshold. A liminal space is
a place in between — a hallway between rooms, a stretch of road between
towns, a coastline between land and water, the hours between night and
morning when most people are asleep.

A surprising fraction of the encounters in this guide happen in liminal
space. The [Black-Eyed Children](/black-eyed-children) appear at car
windows and front doors. The [Beast of Bray Road](/beast-of-bray-road) walks
beside rural roads. The [Mothman](/mothman) was last seen on a bridge, at
night, hours before that bridge collapsed.

Whether this clustering is causal (these places attract such things) or
perceptual (humans are most attentive and most vulnerable in transitional
states), it shows up reliably enough that it functions as a survival rule:
_be most careful at the thresholds._
